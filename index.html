About Me



My full name is Earvin Johnson Jr., but everyone calls me Magic.
I was born on August 14, 1959 so in quick math I'm 62 years old.
I am a lot of things, but mainly - an american former professional basketball player.
I was born in Lansing, Michigan to General Motors assembly worker Earvin Sr. and school janitor Christine.
I have six siblings and three half-siblings from my father's previous marriage.
I was influenced by my parents' strong work ethic. My mother spent many hours after work each night cleaning our home and preparing the next day's meals,
while my father did janitorial work at a used car lot and collected garbage, all while never missing a day at General Motors.
My favorite basketball player was Bill Russell, who I admired for his many championships than his athletic ability.
I was drafted first overall in 1979 by the Los Angeles Lakers. From that moment my life has changed...
Overall, I:

  • scored 17,707 points
  • got 6,559 rebounds
  • helped with 10,141 assists
  • watch my full stats here

My height is 6 ft 9 in (2.06 m) and my weight is 220 lb (100 kg).
After a physical examination before the 1991–92 NBA season, I discovered that I had tested positive for HIV.
In a press conference held on November 7, 1991, I made a public announcement that I would retire immediately and that I would dedicate my life to battle this deadly disease.
I have 2 boys and 1 adopted girl.
I am married to my amazing wife - Cookie.
